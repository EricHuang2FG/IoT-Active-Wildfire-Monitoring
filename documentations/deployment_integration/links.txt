This txt file contains the links to the videos documenting the integration of the deployment.

Video 1: https://drive.google.com/file/d/1B9kVSxmLEpAjHLskNOtt1WgFnR0hwxYz/view?usp=drive_link
This video shows the initial integration of the key servo (DF9GMS) with the deployment mechanism.
due to faulty code integration (servo speed too high), the control was not well configured and the wiring of the servo started
tangling itself. We need to additionally calibrate the code to address this issue.

Video 2: https://drive.google.com/file/d/1NQgJGBopf075Fg2TIalUC3SOWtdoGTqC/view?usp=drive_link
This video shows that after successful calibration, the key lock mechanism on the deployment was able to attach to the 
top of the sensor box. By lifting the key servo mechanism the lid was able to be lifted, proving success in this part of integration.

Video 3: https://drive.google.com/file/d/1bk9nI8Et9fE22TSZefOFPIE9JOT5nB2-/view?usp=drive_link
This video shows the integration of the gear rack to the MG996R servo motor. Integration was successful,
with the electrical and software component the rack was able to move up and down (left or right in the video). 
Controls are successfully done with the push button, as promised in prototype design 04.1.1 software. 

Video 4: https://drive.google.com/file/d/1qpb2zd5Z0qnc0p-z-dHw--lwabcQHpc3/view?usp=drive_link
However, we broke the gear rack briefly after it worked successfully. This prompted us to reglue the rack which provided
a bit of a inconvenience in the process.

Video 5/ photo: it's just photo 9_change... in the same folder:
We ended up burning out our key servo (DF9GMS) due to it being wired to the 5V source from the motor driver. Turns
out it is not supposed to be wired to the 5V source but rather the 3.3V source on the pico board. We switched it before
killing any more servos, although three did perish in the process.

Final video showing both servos working in unison, as controlled by push buttons.
